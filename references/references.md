# Academic References

The sources below were selected because they map directly onto the documented design of PAIciente: executive function, oppositionality, comorbidity, and parent-mediated intervention.

---

## 1. Barkley (1997) — Unified Theory of ADHD

**Citation**
Barkley, R. A. (1997). *Behavioral inhibition, sustained attention, and executive functions: Constructing a unifying theory of ADHD*. *Psychological Bulletin, 121*(1), 65–94.

**Summary**
Seminal article proposing that behavioral inhibition is the core deficit in ADHD and that impairments in working memory, self-regulation of affect/motivation/arousal, internalized speech, and reconstitution follow from it.

**Links**
- DOI: https://doi.org/10.1037/0033-2909.121.1.65
- PubMed: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/9000892/

---

## 2. Barkley (1997) — *ADHD and the Nature of Self-Control*

**Citation**
Barkley, R. A. (1997). *ADHD and the nature of self-control*. Guilford Press.

**Summary**
Book-length expansion of Barkley's self-regulation model of ADHD. Frequently cited in discussions of executive-function delay, behavioral inhibition, and future-directed self-control.

**Links**
- Google Books: https://books.google.com/books/about/ADHD_and_the_Nature_of_Self_Control.html?id=96hkPwAACAAJ

---

## 3. Barkley (2012) — *Executive Functions*

**Citation**
Barkley, R. A. (2012). *Executive functions: What they are, how they work, and why they evolved*. Guilford Press.

**Summary**
Broad theoretical treatment of executive functioning beyond ADHD, useful for grounding self-regulation, planning, motivation, and emotional control in a larger framework.

**Links**
- Publisher: https://www.guilford.com/books/Executive-Functions/Russell-Barkley/9781462545933

## 6. Stringaris & Goodman (2009) — Three Dimensions of Oppositionality

**Citation**
Stringaris, A., & Goodman, R. (2009). *Three dimensions of oppositionality in youth*. *Journal of Child Psychology and Psychiatry, 50*(3), 216–223.

**Summary**
Foundational dimensional paper distinguishing irritable, headstrong, and hurtful forms of oppositionality, with implications for prognosis and treatment response.

**Links**
- DOI: https://doi.org/10.1111/j.1469-7610.2008.01989.x
- PubMed: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/19166573/
- Full text (KCL): https://kclpure.kcl.ac.uk/portal/files/5691300/Stringaris_Goodman_2009.pdf

## 7. Burke et al. (2014) — Irritability Within ODD

**Citation**
Burke, J. D., Boylan, K., Rowe, R., Duku, E., Stepp, S. D., Hipwell, A. E., & Waldman, I. D. (2014). *Identifying the irritability dimension of ODD: Application of a modified bifactor model across five large community samples of children*. *Journal of Abnormal Psychology, 123*(4), 841–851.

**Summary**
Large-sample empirical study supporting irritability as a distinct and clinically meaningful dimension within ODD, while preserving ODD as the broader syndrome.

**Links**
- DOI: https://doi.org/10.1037/a0037898
- PubMed: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/25314267/
- PMC: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4227955/

## 14. Thomas & Zimmer-Gembeck (2007) — PCIT / Triple P Meta-Analysis

**Citation**
Thomas, R., & Zimmer-Gembeck, M. J. (2007). *Behavioral outcomes of Parent-Child Interaction Therapy and Triple P-Positive Parenting Program: A review and meta-analysis*. *Journal of Abnormal Child Psychology, 35*(3), 475–495.

## 15. Murrihy et al. (2023) — CPS vs PMT Randomized Trial

**Citation**
Murrihy, R. C., Drysdale, S. A. O., Dedousis-Wallace, A., Remond, L., McAloon, J., Ellis, D. M., Halldorsdottir, T., Greene, R. W., & Ollendick, T. H. (2023). *Community-delivered Collaborative and Proactive Solutions and Parent Management Training for oppositional youth: A randomized trial*. *Behaviour Therapy, 54*(2), 400–417.

**Summary**
Community-based randomized trial showing that CPS performed comparably to PMT for youth with ODD, with benefits maintained at follow-up.

**Links**
- DOI: https://doi.org/10.1016/j.beth.2022.10.005
- PubMed: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/36858768/

## 16. Dedousis-Wallace et al. (2025) — Predictors and Moderators of PMT vs CPS

**Citation**
Dedousis-Wallace, A., Drysdale, S. A. O., McAloon, J., Murrihy, R. C., Greene, R. W., & Ollendick, T. H. (2025). *Predictors and moderators two treatments of oppositional defiant disorder in children*. *Journal of Clinical Child & Adolescent Psychology, 54*(1), 67–82.

**Summary**
Secondary analysis indicating that treatment fit may differ depending on factors such as parental attributions, inconsistent discipline, and lagging skills.
