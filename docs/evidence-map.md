# Evidence Map — Academic Grounding

The documented behaviors and response rules align with major evidence-informed frameworks in ADHD, oppositional behavior, and parent-mediated intervention. The system does not claim that a single paper "proves" the entire design; rather, it combines several well-established lines of literature.

| Design element | Primary framework | Why it fits | Representative sources |
|---|---|---|---|
| Executive-function emphasis | Barkley's self-regulation and executive-function model | Explains initiation problems, weak response inhibition, sequencing difficulty, and poor time estimation in ADHD. | Barkley 1997; Barkley 2012; Barkley 2015 |
| Emotional lag and the "30% rule" heuristic | Developmental lag framing in ADHD self-regulation literature | Supports the choice to model emotional age as younger than chronological age during demanding interactions. | Barkley 1997/2014 |
| ODD dimensions | Dimensional view of oppositionality | Supports representing irritability, headstrong defiance, and hurtful behavior as partly separable patterns. | Stringaris & Goodman 2009; Burke et al. 2014 |
| Defiance as lagging-skill stress response | Collaborative & Proactive Solutions | Supports treating some oppositional behavior as emerging from lagging skills and unsolved problems rather than simple willful noncompliance. | Greene 2021; Greene & Ablon 2006 |
| Parent coaching and direct behavioral shaping | PCIT and PMT traditions | Supports the use of calm limit-setting, specific praise, scripts, and predictable consequences. | Eyberg/PCIT; Thomas & Zimmer-Gembeck 2007 |
| Family-systems sensitivity | Family-factor and interaction literature | Supports attention to escalation loops, adult intensity, and criticism or coercion effects. | Lin et al. 2022 |
| Need for comprehensive disorder overviews | Disease-primer literature | Provides modern, high-level summaries of ADHD and ODD mechanisms, diagnosis, and intervention. | Faraone et al. 2024; Hawes et al. 2023 |
